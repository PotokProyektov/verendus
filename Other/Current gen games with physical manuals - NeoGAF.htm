Current gen games with physical manuals
I like game manuals. They were an important part of my childhood. I took them to school, read them in bed and pretty much ogled them whenever I could - especially those with nice, color artwork.

These days, most games don't have manuals.



BUT! Some publishers are still choosing to pack in a manual with their games!



(thanks XSEED!)

I wanted to create this thread as a resource for like-minded, manual-loving people who were interested in finding out if a certain game had a physical manual. Obviously, I don't own every current gen game, so I can't make a complete list by myself, so any help adding games is much appreciated!

- Boulder Dash-XL 3D (B/W, 37 pages) Content duplicated over 5 languages
- Kingdom Hearts 3D: Dream Drop Distance (Color, 56 pages). Also comes with AR cards
- One Piece: Unlimited Cruise SP (Color, 43 pages). May feature several languages depending on the region. The Scandinavian version has mainly English but a couple pages in Finnish and Swedish
- One Piece: Unlimited Cruise SP 2 (Color, 42 pages)
- Professor Layton and the Miracle Mask (Color, 29 pages)
- Rhythm Thief & The Emperor's Treasure (B/W, 29 pages) The Scandinavian version has English, and some Swedish and Finnish
- Shin Megami Tensei: Devil Survivor Overclocked (B/W, 38 pages)
- Spirit Camera: The Cursed Memoir (no manual, but comes with a 16-page art booklet)
- Super Street Fighter IV 3D Edition (Color, 61 pages)
- Tales of the Abyss (B/W, 68 pages) Quickstart content duplicated over 8 (!) languages
- Theatrhythm: Final Fantasy (Color, 42 pages)
- Tom Clancy's Ghost Recon: Shadow Wars (B/W, 34 pages)

Edit: Also European Wii U
- Hyrule Warriors (Color, 31 pages)
